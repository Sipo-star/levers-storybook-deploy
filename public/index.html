⚙️ What Is a Lever?

Zara
🤔 "Hey Leo… I keep hearing the word LEVER in Science class. What even IS a lever? Is it just a stick?"
💡 "Great question, Zara! A lever is a simple machine — a rigid bar that pivots around a fixed point to help us move heavy objects with less effort. Yes, a stick CAN be a lever!"
Leo
📐 How a Lever Looks

Fig 1: A lever with all labelled parts

🔍 The Three Main Parts
Fulcrum
The fixed pivot point. The lever rotates around this point. It does not move.
Effort
The force you apply — a push or pull — to make the lever move. Measured in Newtons (N).
Load
The object being moved or lifted. Also called the resistance. Measured in Newtons (N).

AN EXPLANATION OF THE LEVER AS A MECHANICAL SYSTEM

Zara
🔧 "A lever is a mechanical system because it has three stages:
📥 INPUT — you apply an effort force
⚙️ PROCESS — the beam pivots on the fulcrum, transferring the force
📤 OUTPUT — the load is moved or lifted

It transforms a small force over a long distance into a larger force over a shorter distance!"
Key Principles: Fulcrum · Effort · Load

🔵 Fulcrum

The fixed pivot point around which the lever rotates. It is the support that stays completely still and allows the beam to turn. E.g., the centre bolt of scissors, or the wheel of a wheelbarrow.

🟢 Effort

The input force you apply to the lever — a push or pull — to overcome the load. Effort is measured in Newtons (N). The further your effort is from the fulcrum, the less effort you need!

🟠 Load

The output force — the weight or resistance being moved by the lever. Also measured in Newtons (N). In a wheelbarrow, the bricks are the load. In your arm, the object in your hand is the load.
⚡ "Now let me explain FORCE and MECHANICAL ADVANTAGE!

Force is any push or pull measured in Newtons (N). On a lever, you apply effort force — and the lever produces a load force on the other side.

Mechanical Advantage (MA) tells us HOW MUCH the lever multiplies your effort:
📐 MA = Load ÷ Effort
If MA = 3, you only need 1 N of effort to lift 3 N of load!"
Leo
Key Principles: Force · Mechanical Advantage

🔴 Force

A push or pull that changes the motion or shape of an object. Force is measured in Newtons (N). On a lever: your muscles apply effort force; the lever produces load force as output.

🟣 Mechanical Advantage (MA)

MA = Load ÷ Effort
Tells us how much the lever multiplies your force:
• MA > 1 → easier to lift (less effort needed) ✅
• MA = 1 → no advantage, effort = load
• MA < 1 → more effort, but gain speed ⚡

EXAMPLES OF EACH TYPE OF LEVER IN DAILY LIFE, SHOWN THROUGH YOUR CHARACTERS USING THEM IN REAL-LIFE CONTEXTS

Seesaw at the Park

🔵 Fulcrum: centre post (middle)

🟦 Load: friend on the other side
⚡ MA: can be >1, =1 or <1

⚡ MA always < 1 — gains speed & range!
